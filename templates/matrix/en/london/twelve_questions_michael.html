Michael Sebastian

Profile and Leo's Take
Age：23　Years in London: 3 Months

Michael was one of my roommates in London. We lived in one of those multiple room flats catered to Airbnb in this day and age, where people are coming and leaving all the time. Michael, Willem (who’ll come in a special Brussels version), and I were the few long-term stays. Michael also was the only one there who actively engaged in some sort of social conversation lol. So the socialising went on, mostly between Michael, me, and one of Michael’s girl-friends who ran away from her home in Copenhagen and joined the flat midway through my stay. The two of them created a pretty interesting perception of Danish people for me—especially after one night when I was invited to a pub nearby. I arrived late, around 11pm and the two of them were playing a drinking game (normal), where the loser had to answer a question given by the winner, on lighthearted topics such as “Is religion more beneficial or harmful to society” (Yeah, not so normal). I also wandered upon them in the kitchen one morning reading and discussing Oscar Wilde. Yeah, so as you probably would assume, Michael’s answers are interesting. I’d recommend the place and thing (Q.3), the flag (Q.4), the future (Q.6), and his moments (Q.11). So Michael, see you around, and we can have more fun conversations in Tokyo next time!!

I think I would like to travel to a quiet and isolated place. Maybe, one of the small, not too touristy Greek islands. Just a place that offered space for reflection and relaxation. That'd be good!

>>>Get out of the hectic, modern lifestyle.

Exactly! I think it's pretty natural to long for these places when you're used to living in the city. So, yeah.


Q2
What country do you feel “close” to, other than your own?

A2
Okay. I think I'll go… I'll go with Holland. To be more specific, Amsterdam.

>>>Why?

Well, because It feels like my second home. And I don't have to do anything particular. Just sitting next to the canals outside a cafe while I’m soaking up some sunshine makes me feel relaxed. I think it's the atmosphere and the attitude of the people… and yeah. It seems like a good place for me. I would like to eventually move there, at some point.



Q3
What one "thing" and "place" best represents your city?

A3
Can the thing and the place be the same?

>>>Yes, they can be the same.

I would say the Millennium Bridge. Because, in a way, it connects the past with the present. The past being St. Paul's Cathedral, on the north bank. The present cathedral was built between 1675 and 1710. That place seems to…(thinking)… it has held many important services, like the funerals of Lord Nelson and Sir Winston Churchill and the jubilee celebrations for Queen Victoria. I think it is a very important place in terms of the country’s national identity. St Paul’s is the symbol of the past.

The present is symbolized by Tate Modern, which is located on the south bank of the River Thames, on the other side of bridge. It holds this amazing collection of contemporary art from the 1900s up to the present day. And it seems to be one of the city’s main cultural centers where people of all ages and from all cultural backgrounds gather to look at great works of art. I think I’m a bit biased by choosing this museum as a place. speaker 2: We are all biased in our own ways and that’s what makes us interesting.

If I had to redesign the Danish flag?

Well, if it had to symbolize the youth of Denmark, I would paint it all black. I would maybe put some white sneakers in the middle of the flag. (Leo laughs) Because everyone seems to wear long coats including myself and sneakers. Well, I don’t wear sneakers yet, but yeah.

Q6
How do you feel about the future? Both yourself and mankind.

A6
Okay. (Thinking)… Everything seems to be going very fast these days in terms of technological innovation. And I am not sure if it is all for the better. In terms of social media technologies which were supposed to bring us closer to each other, it seems to have had quite the opposite effect, everyone seems be…hmm, in the hunt for more recognition and acknowledgement. And I definitely think that this focus on the ’self’ rather than the Other has some negative consequences. Well, for example, the feeling of loneliness is a topic I have discussed with many of my friends. It is definitely a symptom of this age. People are not getting more connected with each other in real life. But I definitely think there will be a reaction against the so-called social medias at some point, hopefully. But yeah. Future’s uncertain.

Q11
What was the happiest moment in your life? What was the most terrifying moment in your life?

A11
Well, I don’t think the happiest moments in my life are bounded to a specific event or situation. I think it’s more like a state of mind where I forget myself and become one with the thing I’m doing. And that can be planning exhibitions, playing music, seeing friends or family. The moments where I’m usually most happy are the ones where I feel most inspired and creative.

>>> Can you give us an example?

When I first arrived at the V&A, one week before I started my internship, it almost felt like a religious experience, walking through these incredible exhibition spaces with cultural heritage from all around the world. And by the time I reached the room with the Raphael Cartoons, I almost shed a tear, which has never happened to me before when looking at a work of art. So…just the thought that I was going to be a part of this incredible place was definitely something that made me feel happy.

>>>Very, very cool. What was the most terrifying moment?

I guess it relates to my happiest moments because I’m definitely most terrified when I have lost the sense of inspiration, or meaning. I guess…, when I was seven I was chased by a pitbull (Leo laughs) and that was not a pleasant experience either. But I guess in general it is when I have lost the sense of inspiration. I’m actually kind of worried about that.


Q12
What was your childhood dream? What would you say to the childhood you, now? And what is your dream now?

A12
My childhood dream was to become a mountain climber because I was very good at climbing trees. So, that was the next step. Well, I thought so back then. In my primary school I wanted to pursue a career as a film director, and an actor, and an author. So, in that sense, I think I have always been drawn to the creative industries. My aunt used to say that I had an ”eclectic range of interests”. That expression basically meant that she thought I was talented in more creative fields but not talented enough to turn them into a career. (Leo laughs). That made me work even harder to obtain my goals. So, I’m very thankful for her “encouragement”. Definitely. In a way she has fueled my desire to go into the art industries. Because I wanted to disprove her. That being said, I have a close bond with my aunt.

>>>What would you say to your childhood you then?

(Thinking)…I would probably say that you shouldn’t do something you hate just because other people are doing it. Like playing soccer. I used to play soccer in primary school… and I just did it because I wanted to fit in with the groups of boys in my class. And it wasn’t very fulfilling at all and I played soccer for five years. Now, I love playing soccer, but at that time I wasn’t very interested in it. And… yeah. I would tell my younger self not to pretend to like something that you hate just to fit into a certain group, you’ll end up hating yourself for it.

>>>All right. And finally, what is your dream now?

My dream? I guess it would be to further develop my electic range of interests (Laughs) and… to give people experiences that are interesting and beneficial.”
