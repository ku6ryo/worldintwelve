Profile and Leo's Take
Age：27　Years in London: 5 years

Roberto was introduced to me by Sayaka. Roberto is just one of those guys that oozes “nice dude”-ness, and he was just that and so much more. Roberto first invited me to do some climbing with him in North London, at this location called The Castle (which was literally a castle filled with rock-climbing walls!). I was so out of shape I think I ended up pulling something, but it was a new experience. Then we went and had a burger, which was also an adventure because everywhere Roberto wanted to go was closed so we did a lot of roaming around Angel lol. It was also inspiring talking with Roberto on a variety of topics, ranging from five day diving trips in the Red Sea off Egypt where you’re living on a boat, to childhood memories in Italy, to being in cross-cultural romantic relationships. Yeah, good times! Roberto gave great, extensive answers to all questions, but if I were to recommend, they would be the month “Q.1”, the images “Q.5”, the partner “Q.8”, and children “Q.9”. So Roberto I will see you around, and I know you’ll come to Tokyo again! Or I can meet you somewhere around the world with Sayaka :)

Q8
What do you look for in a partner?

A8
(Thinking)…Playfulness, happiness, and to a degree, common interests.

>>>We did talk about this. Those common interests, you said it’s okay if there’s a few things you don’t have in common with another person.

I think it’s tricky. I think that one of the beauties of a partner is spending time with the other person. And I think to do that, it helps obviously if you both enjoy doing similar things. So I think there’s a degree of independence that you both need in regards to doing your own thing and being independent. Being comfortable with your own time. And when you spend time together, it’s doing something that you both enjoy doing and that you can connect to. So I find shared interests to be a lovely aspect of our relationship. So what I look for in a girlfriend is yeah. Is there an element of shared interests. So at the end of the day, if you’re just not spending any time with your partner, then it’s a no. Different people, different relationships. But my margin of threshold in terms of time spent is some time alone and some time with them. So with that you want to enjoy the time you spend with each other.
