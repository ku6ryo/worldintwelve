Profile and Leo's Take
Age：26　Years in Berlin: 5

Jen was introduced to me through Genevieve. Jen is one of those thinker types—people I can relate with instantly because that’s what I consider myself to be. The thinker types usually do a lot of questioning (hence they’re thinkers) and it was actually kind of crazy because Jen actually re-questioned the first two questions of the interview haha. It was certainly a first. As you may have expect, I had an incredible time conversing with Jen, and we actually touched on a topic that has stuck with me even as I’m writing this. One of the most thought provoking incidents I’ve encountered in my year, it was regarding Jen’s work at the time consulting school teachers, and involving religious children and their hardcore (in this case extreme) faith at such a young age. It got me thinking about what it is to live in Europe, to be situated on a continent literally connected to hundreds of other nations (in some cases completely different culturally and economically), and that impact that has on not just your society, but even yourself, the individual. Yep. Deep stuff. So, again, as you might expect, Jen gave amazing answers. Every single one is good, and I would recommend you read them all, but if pressed for time, I would choose closeness (Q.2), images (Q.5), important thing (Q.7), and dreams (Q.12). So Jen, see you around, when I get back to Berlin!! I’m looking forward to more stimulating and insightful discussions! Yay!!

(Thinking)…I don’t think there is one. I find it strange.
