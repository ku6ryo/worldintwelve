Arielle V

Profile and Leo's Take
Age：26　Years in New York： 6 months

Arielle was introduced to me through Jennifer. Arielle, an art grad-student also working in an auction house, is one of those figures with an amazing sense of humor. You can tell this by reading her answers; she always has that one word or combination of words that gives an idea or thought that unique touch. I could also tell that Arielle has a very strong sense of her values, which I believe is one of the most important traits for a friend, and it was a lot of fun talking with her about art and the auction business (which I knew nothing about) at her favourite dive bar, amazing rooftop, and even her wonderful cozy home. For her answers, my favourites were the representation (Q.3), the flag (Q.4), her images (Q.5), and her partner (Q.8). Absolutely hilarious! So Arielle, thanks for a wonderful time, and I think I’ll be contacting you again because I have some interesting things I’m planning to do!! Haha

Q4
If you were to redsign your country's flag, how would you do it?

A4
Would I do it? No. I'm not really a fan of symbols, they're kind of useless after a point to me. I mean, I am studying contemporary art, so I have all of these theories that I can't shut off. So I wouldn’t… because it's a meaningless symbol, essentially.

>>>The flag is?

The flag is, to me. If there was an ideal, if this was me six years ago as a Freshman going to college and studying art, it would be something very fluid, kind of like a Rothko fuzzy edges and no hard lines but… time changes all of that. So I'm not really sure if I really have an answer for what my design would look like now. (laughs)

Q11
What was the happiest moment in your life? What was the most terrifying moment in your life?

A11
I would say that the happiest moments that I've had… aside from getting into undergrad and getting into grad school…(Thinking) I was working at a non profit last summer and my kids, my participants—we call them “participants”— were putting up their art show, and most of them had just never painted before. So seeing how excited they were and their pride and seeing that they “made something”, that they could see the results of what they made. That's probably one of the happiest moments that I’ve had. That meant a lot to me.

For the other…I would say a good chunk of my teen years between 15 and the day I left for… well when I continued on. Just turmoil in my family, for several years in my life, it was just kind of constant. Not always one particular thing but just a constant state of THAT in Kansas. …Well I mean I also have some kind of funny terrifying moments.

>>>Can you give us one?

I almost got taken once. like Liam Neeson “Taken”, it felt like, in Shanghai. And the ATM was in Mandrin and I was trying to switch it to English and I was just flustered and I start the process all over again and the first thing that it said on the screen once i got it switched over to English was "Do not be distracted by people while using this ATM". (Leo laughs) So I'm like “Well, this is the end. Alright, it's been great.” (Leo Laughs) So I just took out the smallest amount of cash that I could because this guy was following me at this point.
