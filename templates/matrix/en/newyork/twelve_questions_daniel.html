Daniel Weschler

Profile and Leo's Take
Age：26　Years in New York： 4 years

Dan I actually met as a child when we visited the East Coast. My father was a friend of Dan’s mother, and while the adults did their talking, we children chased fireflies. Good times. Dan is part of PigPen Theater Co. which means he lives one of the coolest lives one can imagine. Some lucky people get to live by creating and acting in theater. Other lucky people get to live by creating and performing music in a band. Dan gets to do both. And you can see why. Dan is smart, funny, and a super nice guy--the trifecta for success!! Haha. We didn’t have the best luck finding places to eat while near his pad and studio in Brooklyn, but still it was a great time! PigPen’s basement just oozed with creative energy and...ok I’ll admit it. I’m jealous of their lifestyle lol. Dan, with his creativity and sense of humor gave great answers, all of which I recommend, but if I had to choose, I would say the flag (Q.4), the images (Q.5), partner (Q.8) which was one of the coolest ones I’ve gotten, and finally the dream (Q.12). So Dan, I know we’ll meet again, and do get your band on World tour!! Let’s meet up somewhere haha, but the US works too :)

That sounds great. I'd love to do that for a month.

Q4
If you were to redsign your country's flag, how would you do it?

A4
Hmm…(Thinking)… I think it could use a redesign. I don't know if I'm the one to do it. I don't claim to be a designer or a . . . what do you call it, a vexillologist, a flag person, but I feel like almost any country, after about 100 years, could dispense with all the symbols associated with it. Because they're so fraught with so many generally terrible things---kind of inevitably---that I think it's not a bad idea to switch out the representation every now and then. The American Flag . . . I don't know, red, white, and blue just feels boring. (Chuckles) Probably because it's the flag I've been seeing in the primary school classroom for my entire life. So, maybe we should do something like super translucent or something no one's ever done before. Cutting edge. Like, Google should design a flag (Leo laughs) or… a translucent fiber screen and it has the same GIF playing on loop of a bald eagle. Yeah, I think that would be great.

>>>This was definitely the most high-tech one I've gotten.

Oh, yeah?

>>>I mean I was in San Francisco and no one gave me any of these high-tech answers.

Oh, really. That's a shame, they should be way on top of that.

>>>It's called imagination.

Yeah, that's right.

Q6
How do you feel about the future? Both yourself and mankind.

A6
(Takes deep breath) I feel like I'm more optimistic for my own future than I am for the future of humanity as a whole. But, also maybe . . . I don't know. There's nothing, you know, like any documentary you watch or article you read tells you it's way too late to stop the coming of global catastrophe---global warming and stuff. All of the scientists have this kind of like gleeful “zen” about it where they're all just like, “Yeah it's too late for us, but you know what, that's nature.” And, it's maybe a little cowardly of me, but I guess, like everyone else, I just think it's not going to happen in my lifetime. But, when I think about my own future I think about being able to do what you're doing. Being able to travel, more freedom, which again is kind of optimistic because...in general, I think the older you get more responsibility and it shackles you to various things. Not necessarily in a bad way, but we'll see. I should probably do all my traveling now is the upshot of all of that. (Laughs)

>>>Well, you do go on tour, so that’s some traveling.

That's true. I want to go to Europe and Asia, and all that stuff. So…we'll have to compare notes before I take my 12 month trip.

Q11
What was the happiest moment in your life? What was the most terrifying moment in your life?

A11
Some of the happiest moments in my life have been performing, playing in a concert to a sold-out crowd. We did a show in Chicago on our last tour, at Lincoln Hall, that was the closest thing to a rock star experience I think any of us have ever had, and it was great. It was a drug.

>>>How many people came to the venue?

It was 600.

>>>Oh, that's a lot.

Yes. So, it's enough to…well, you're like “Look at all these people!” When you're doing something you love, you're sort of blasting past the usual insecurities (Leo bursts into laughter) and the voice in your head shuts up for five minutes and that can be an amazing moment.

But also, times with loved ones. I remember I was really sick, when I graduated from college. I was really sick for a couple of months and then my sister got married. I was just sort of recovering and I was still kind of weak, but it was that kind of… cleansed feeling that you get from being sick for a long time and then getting to leave your bed for the first time in awhile. And, just being at my sister's wedding with my girlfriend and a lot of my friends, and seeing my dad dance for the first time, it was a real feeling, like I had a real community of friends and family. That was one of the high points for sure.

Then, the scariest...(Thinking)…I mean, probably just when I was a kid, times when I thought I saw a ghost or something. It was literally the scariest. Yeah, I mean things like that… all of the fears of childhood I think had a pretty big impact. But since then everything's been less scary and more anxiety-filled. Which, I don't know which of those I prefer. I guess they both have their uses, I guess.


Q12
What was your childhood dream? What would you say to the childhood you, now? And what is your dream now?

A12
Well, I think I wanted to live adventures and I'd create larger than life experiences for myself. Which, I think drove me towards acting. Just growing up in the woods, running around, and hitting trees with sticks and pretending they were giants and stuff. I think at some point, that very childlike dream turned into, “well, there's a way I can do this and get to pretend all these incredible things.” Then at some point I think that turns into—the drudgery of reality turns into an engagement with reality and that's really what theatre became for me. A really great way into seeing the world through that lens and not having to make it up as much. But, just looking at things in a way where you feel that sense of adventure and excitement.

My dream now… I mean I'm really getting to do a lot of the stuff I wanted to do. I definitely never dreamed I was going to be able to tour in a band and play music that I loved. Well, at least not at this point and this far into my life. And I'm getting to do that. Really, the next part of my dream just entails making enough money to be able to keep living my dream. Because, I do think I'm living my dream, and I'm just waiting for the other shoe to drop.

>>>What would you tell your child in you now? Would you say, in a way I am doing what you were thinking you want to do?

Yeah, I would say that and I would also say, learn discipline, or learn discipline sooner. (Both laugh) Because, I think I spent a good chunk of my life not really educating myself. I think I reached a certain point and I felt like I knew enough. There's a long three year fall where I realized I know absolutely nothing. I could have spent a lot of that time learning more, instead of crowding for the rope as it's flying through my hands. I would just say “study up kid.”
