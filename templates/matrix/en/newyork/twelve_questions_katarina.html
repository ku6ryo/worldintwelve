Katarina Pasinsky

Profile and Leo's Take
Age：25　Years in New York： 1 1/2

Katarina was introduced to me through Nana. What I found truly interesting about Katarina was her background--being half Hungarian half Korean, schooling in Utah, college in LA, studying abroad in various European cities, now in NY--and through those experiences, her views towards life and education (being a teacher). Katarina showed me around Williamsburg where she used to live, and the location, Bakery, was an amazing cafe I really recommend. Katarina's answers were very elequent, concise, and to the point--it seemed she chose every word knowing it would be the perfect one to portray the picture in her mind. I recommend her close country (Q.2), her "thing"--one of the mose unique answers I've gotten (Q.7), her moments (Q.11), and her dream (Q.12). So see you again Katarina, whether it be in Europe or US or Asia! We will continue to be the children of the world!!

Q3
What one "thing" and "place" best represents your city?

A3
I'll start with the place because it's the first thing I can think of (Laughs). I'd say… Central Park. And I don't actually think that it has a great representation of just Manhattan in general, but I do think that the foundation of building a park where people in the city can gather and where there's no barrier to entry and that actually covers so much of the city is huge in and of itself. I also think that when I think of New York, I think of people wanting to visit. I see it as a very transient city, constantly moving, and I think that Central Park draws a lot of people. So although it might not be locals all of the time, I think that a lot of Manhattan is people visiting Manhattan, and I think Central Park would sort of represent that. But it's interesting because I haven't actually lived in Manhattan. I've only lived in Brooklyn, so I think the question would be different if it was framed like, what is the one place in Brooklyn that represents New York.

>>>Do you have a thing?

A thing? But I actually think the subway. Its…It is the vehicle that connects the web of people and places in New York, and I think that it's incredibly important and makes New York what it is.
