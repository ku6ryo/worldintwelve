Lily Mathias

Profile and Leo's Take
Age：30　Years in Rio de Janeiro： 2

Lily was introduced to me through Pedro. Lily is one of those people that you would define with a bunch or “e” adjectives—eccentric, electrifying, full or energy and enjoying life to the fullest. She has done her world travelling, she has worked in various fields I have never even imagined existed, and most of all, she is all over the place! I met Lily a few times while in Rio, mostly with Roberta and Pedro (as you can see in the pictures), but I had to consider myself lucky. If there was anytime off, whether it be afterwork on a weekday, or a long weekend, Lily was out, everywhere! Even while I was there, Lily did an early morning hike to the outskirts or Rio (as you can see in pic.1), and took a trip to the centre of Brazil to check out a famous lagoon (as you can see in pics. 4,5). So yeah, basically talking and being with Lily makes you think “Holy…I got to get my shit together“ in the best way possible :). All of Lily’s answers are great, but if I were to choose, I’d say closeness (Q.2), future (Q.6), children (Q.9), and moments (Q.11). So Lily, thanks again, and I will see you around! You’ll be travelling again I’m sure, and we’ll meet up!!

Q6
How do you feel about the future? Both yourself and mankind.

A6
I would be concerned about the future...I could say all of mankind. I think that’s the point I’m more scared about. I’m more skeptical about it. But it has a lot to do with me as well. I wonder where our generation is headed. And especially the new, the earlier, more of the newborn generations. It’s very provocative how sometimes I see a lot of changes coming while on the other hand, I think we’re going on a path that I don’t know how—I’m not confident it’s a sustainable path. And that has totally to do with—I could say mankind—but it has a lot to do with me. So how you deal with work, career, and how you deal with your time and how many hours you’re connected and how relationships have changed.

I’ve been through things that made me see how important it is for you to disconnect and disattach from a steady and safe life. And still, when you’re back, it’s so hard to let go. So it is all about the same thing in the end. Like, it’s all how you’re not living the relationships as you should. You’re not having as much free time as you should. And it’s the same personal drama that I see for mankind. If we’re truly connected to one another nature-wise…like you do when you’re traveling. When I was traveling, I was absolutely safe of what I was doing and where I was going. I said “this is what I should do for my life and this is what mankind should do”. They should be interacting with other people, to respect the difference in culture. They should be admiring nature and everything because that will make them more respectful and more sustainable.

So when we’re doing what you’re doing [Note: in this case travelling], you’re living the life to say “okay, I’m doing the right thing here”. But when you’re back to your life and routine and career that you start working to maintain a lifestyle and you question this lifestyle and it’s all wrong. And you don’t have a relationship like you should. You’re not so cautious as you should be, and this kind of thing. So I think those are my worries about the future. It’s mankind and it’s who I am. Species.

Q11
What was the happiest moment in your life? What was the most terrifying moment in your life?

A11
That’s so hard. Well definitely traveling, that’s no doubt. But it’s hard to point one moment. (Thinking)…I always say that a happy trip, like a perfect trip, it’s not like the entire trip was perfect. The whole thing, but those ten minutes you have or even ten seconds that you have—this breeze come in. Or this scene or this moment that makes you feel absolutely grateful for your existence. That’s worth your entire trip. And in my one year trip, I probably have ten moments of those but it makes the whole thing worth it.

>>>I relate to that very much.

Isn’t it? It’s not like every moment you’re absolutely happy in a place. That’s never true. Every place I’ve been to, even if it’s absolutely special, it’s not like you’re happy 100 percent of the time. If there’s even one moment that will make you remember this place, you’re gonna remember “I was happy there. I was happy at that trip”. And I had plenty of those moments and I can name a few. I can never say one place. I think the place I was closest to for a long period I was really happy—Southeast Asia had some things. Because it has the whole thing. It’s a cultural experience. You’re actually very close to locals so you’re not feeling very arrogant about anything. You feel like you actually have your feet on the ground there and at the same time it’s not… Well you have nature everywhere. You feel very happy about yourself. It’s crazy.

There was one second that I remember saying “okay, my entire existence is to be here in this place” - having this feeling. And I was crossing the Mekong River for two days and in one of those moments, there was this special light and all these people in the boat were absolutely connected and I was so happy and thankful. And that’s one thing. The place that I pray the most and I’m very grateful, is always when I’m traveling and I have this very majestic experience. So I was telling you about my trip to [Lagoon area in central Brazil] and I have these two moments there that probably last less than one minute and I say okay, “I’ll remember feeling that happy every time I remember that place.” So there are a number of situations on a list of countries and places I felt like that. But definitely related to travel.

>>>What was terrifying?

(Thinking)…I think…(thinking)…I don’t know how to explain it in English but I think it’s kind of a human despair. When you feel very suffocated. Kind of a panic attack. I never had something that I could actually qualify as that but it’s always related when you’re very suffocated about travel, about work, and about money and how that’s so big in your life. And I have moments like that. Before I travel, after I travel, and every time that that happens. And I’m so scared. It’s not about death or losing someone. It’s just about…I’m not in my most balanced moment here and that’s absolutely terrifying. It’s not about violence or even life risk when you’re traveling and you have something really scary happen. None of that. But being in a place where you’re absolutely scared and you can’t live with yourself. That’s the most terrifying thing. And when I get there, I say “okay, you have to stop whatever you’re doing and get out of this situation. This is not healthy.” And I’d say I’m very centered to this… just okay, stop whatever you’re doing and get out of here. But I’ve been there so I would say that.
