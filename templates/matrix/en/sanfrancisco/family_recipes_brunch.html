San Francisco × Brunch Culture

Overview


Leo loves to eat, so he is doing various food related sub-projects in each city. For this one, he chooses a topic and does a little hands-on, "field work". Basically he goes to various resturants and eats!! Hurrah!!


Theme


So for San Francisco, I chose "Brunch Culture". I'm assuming the concept of brunch exists basically everywhere around the world, however I've never seen it so prominent as in San Francisco. Basically, brunch is like a religion. You have to eat it on the weekends. It's part of the "type of living" that fits in right with juicing, jogging, dog walking, and other themes in San Francisco. It's a necessity to start off your day on the right foot, and eating a nice brunch is just that. All three of my roommates ate brunch almost every Saturday and Sunday, either with girlfriends, groups of friends, or even with dates found on an app!! So I decided to do the same, and went to a few popular places.


My first brunch experience was "MyMy's Coffee Shop". It was a recommended by my brunch loving roommate Pat and his foodie girlfriedn Saniya. They said this place serves some of the best pancakes in San Francisco. So I arrived around 1:30 pm, not even brunch time anymore, and yet there were still people in line!

Final Thoughts


So thats it. To tell you the truth, I had a few other, not as extensive brunches other than this, and after the whole ordeal my honest opinion was "I've had enough brunch for a while" haha. Yeah, brunch is nice on a Saturday or Sunday, not 3 times a week!! A few points below.

1. This is obvious for people who know brunch, but brunch is all about "Sweet and Savory". As you can tell, I usually had a sweet main (pancakes, french toast, etc.) and a savory side (bacon, sausages, etc.). But you can also have the opposite, a savory main (Eggs Benedict, Soufule, etc.), and have a sweet side (danish, toast, etc.). Also, the fact that every place serves eggs to your desire is great (sunny side up, scrambles, poached, ect.), so you can always get the ultimate combination of sweet+savory+melts-in-your-mouth!!

2. So I know that many other cities in the US and the world have places that serve brunch, but I've never seen it so prevalent throughout the daily lives of everybody. They have to have it on the weekends, as seen with "Sweet Maple" where people wait for 3 hours to get into a place that requires no wait on the weekdays. Its so important to them to eat on the weekend, and I think that's the essence of brunch culture in San Francisco. The end.
