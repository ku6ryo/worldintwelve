Place: Subterranean Art House Date: 1/25/2014

The last Monday of January, 2015. I was at the "Subterranean Art House", an event space for art and culture in Berkeley which I had visited the previous week. Rhishikesh Limaye, one of the curators said to me, "You should use this space for you Treasure Box project" and I was more than delighted. After that, with the help of one of my "12Qs" interviewees Catlin, we started finding artists who might want to participate. Rhishi even did some PR on the Art House's wbsite!!



I found one participant, and Catlin found two. I was actually overbooked!! I couldn't believe it until...



No one showed up. Well, to be honest, I got a text from my participant the previous night that her work shift had been rescheduled and she wouldn't be able to make it. As for Catlin's friends, one was really excited for the project until he woke up the morning of the event and decided "he wanted to be by himself" that day, and the other was actually physically with Catlin that morning until he told her he needed to go home once, left for a BART station, and now was unreachable. Hmmm.


After an hour or so sitting in an empty space with Catlin, chatting about this an that, "Treasure Box Battle: San Francisco" met its destiny: cancellation. I was thinking of all the things I should have done (which I'll point out at the end of this segment), when Catlin told me she would show me around Oakland, where there was one, Burning Man statues, and two, a house where her friend lived with a few artists. Final Thoughts


As shown above, "Treasure Box Battle: San Francisco" did never come to fruition. So I'd just finish off by making a couple of points on what I learned from this experience.

1. "Understand which type of artist I'm dealing with"： So the entirety of the "World in Twelve" relies on asking peole who I've never met to take time out of their lives to help me out on my personal project. And since I'm not paying anybody anything, the only thing that holds together me and that person is trust. I'm not saying that artists in general are lacking in that department, but its true that say, they are less likely to be in constant contact than businessmen lol. What's important to understand is, even within the wide category of "artist", there are the people who are paying their bills with their art, and there are the types that are well, lets just say "fine artists". The former, I've had no problem corresponding with in any of my projects, but the latter, they do have their spontaneous "want to be alone days", etc. I mean, this wasn't the first time I've been put off. Anyway, going along for the rest of the year, I'll have to be careful in tryin to choose the former type for help on my project.
2. "Try to be in constant contact"： However, saying that, its going to be impossible to only get the former type. I'll just try to be in constant contact with artists, sending reminders and such. This time might have turned out different if I had done more of this.

So that's it. Let's hope Toronto works out!!
