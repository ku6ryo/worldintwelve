Q1
If you were handed US$2500 and received a one month vacation where you could live in one city, anywhere in the world, where would you choose? What would you do?

A1
Well since I'm pregnant now, a month would be around when I'll be giving birth. But say I weren't pregnant, I'd probably like to go to France again. France is my husband's father's homeland, and it also happens to be the first country that I went abroad. And I really fell in love with the country--of course, at the time I couldn't imagine I would one day marry a frenchman--but now my friends give the "Uh huh, coincidence, riiiiight" reaction all the time! haha.
　　 When I visit France this time, I'd like to try to view the city and country through my step-father and mother's eyes. Last time I was just sight seeing, but this time, I'd like to try to walk through the city as I walk through my hometown.


Q2
What country do you feel “close” to, other than your own?

A2
I haven't really been to many countries, but when I talk to people from different countries, I feel like each person has their own similarities and differences. However, I do believe Parisians are similar to Tokyoites, in many ways. Parisians really love and are proud of the city and area they live in. Also the fact that there is a vast difference in the people who are native Parisians, and people who came from elsewhere, I belieive is really similar to Tokyo. Some of the natives are extremely conservative and excluding, but others are very open and welcoming, and the people from elsewhere don't really care and happily live their daily lives.
　 Also, the fact that there are historical monuments throughout the city, like this shrine, is also very similar. Especially the fact that to tourists, this is an attraction, but to the locals, its just normal life. And the fact that the locals use it as an opportunity for business is also very similar, I believe.

Q4
What one place and thing best represents your city?

A4
Hmmm...(thinks for a bit)...as an area, I believe this Yanesen area best represents Tokyo. Of course, if I were taking around tourists from foreign countries I'd take them to Shibuya, Aasakusa, the Tokyo Skytree, etc, but I truly believe this Yanesen area has it all too. There are old places and new places, all mixed together. I believe a city will slowly start to die out unless it is constantly evolving, bringing in new ideas, and I think the Yanesen area is doing a great job of this. Its very "Iki"(粋)！(A traditional Japanese word meaning "Stylish, smart, chic, fashionable, etc.")　
　 As for a "thing"...hmmm...maybe, buildings? Real estate? I don't think there are many cities like Tokyo, even throughout the world, where you go two train stations down and there is a completely different city-scape. Shibuya, Shinjuku, Asakusa--all the buildings are so different. Its like there are a bunch of small cities in this giant megalopolis known as Tokyo.

Q11
What was your childhood dream? What would you say to the childhood you, now?

A11
I wanted to become a lawyer when I was young. That's what I wrote in school, and answered to anyone who asked, but to tell the truth, I really had no idea what a lawyer was! haha. I just had the image that it was someone that protected people. And eventually, still without really knowing what a lawyer was, I enterend university and majored in law. That's when I realized, wow, law is boring! So if I could say something to the childhood me, it would be this: don't say you want to become something when you have no idea what that is! Just say, a florist or something! haha.


Q12

I have two. One is to raise this child to become a respected adult, and have a happy family.
