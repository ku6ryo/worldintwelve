Björn Stampes

Profile and Leo's Take
Age：50　Years in Stockholm: 30+

Bjorn was introduced to me through Carin. Bjorn is and artist, and was super nice showing me around various parts of Sodermalm plus taking me out for brunch. He also invited me to a party where one of his relatives was doing an art show at a restaurant, which also was a unique experience. One thing I really didn’t imagine before I started this project was the vast diversity in age of the people I would meet, and talking with Bjorn really illuminated this fact. It was fun hearing about Stockholm (and Denmark too for that matter) through a different perspective than say the people I met in their 20s, and even the various people I met in their 30s and 40s. Bjorn was every bit what I imagined a Swedish artist to be: intellectual, soft-spoken, and selective of his thoughts and words. For the 12 questions I would recommend his representations (Q.3), children (Q.9), and dreams (Q.12). So Bjorn, thanks again, and I know you’ll come to Japan once more, so until then!! :)

Check out Bjorn's work at bjornstampes.com/works. Bjorn even had an exhibit in Japan!!

Q1
If you were handed US$2500 and received a one month vacation where you could live in one city, anywhere in the world, where would you choose? What would you do?

A1
Oh, I’d go to Tokyo.

>>>Really?

Yeah.

>>>Again?

Yeah! I wanna go back!

>>>Was that your first time going to Tokyo? That last time?

No, second. Yeah, but I was in Tokyo 20 years ago now. So…

>>>Oh, I see. Things have changed.

Yeah. Really? To the better, I think. Well, I really liked it. We were there for two and a half weeks. It’s great. Very interesting.

>>>What would you do? What would you like to do?

(Thinking) Well, what would I like? Well, I like that it’s big.

>>>Coming from Stockholm.

Yeah. (Leo laughs) Yes and I kind of like the pulse, actually. Maybe you’re not so fond of it?

>>>Well, I like, I love Tokyo. I can’t complain.

Yeah? No, but I like the tempo. I like… so many things to see. So very different from being here in many ways. In other ways, it’s very similar. It’s very interesting. Socially, architecturally. Anyway so I would like to go back.

Q3
What one "thing" and "place" best represents your city?

A3
Like a phenomena or?

>>>It can be anything. Anything and any place.

(Thinking)… Well…. I guess, for me, Stockholm at its best, it’s when you walk home on a summer night. That’s really, really beautiful.

>>>That’s such a cool answer.

And I don’t know if that’s a thing. Or it’s not a place.

>>>That’s a great. No, it’s a thing. It’s a thing, for sure. Wow. That’s gotta be one of the cooler answers I’ve gotten. What about a place? Is there a place?

A place that Stockholm? (Thinking) Oh, that’s harder. Oh, I don’t have a good answer to that, actually. (Leo laughs) I don’t know if this is where we are now, is this “Stockholm”? No, not, perhaps. It’s one of the better parts of Stockholm, I guess.

>>>Can you give us a place that you think is a great place to walk back home on a summer night instead?

Yeah. Go, if you walk from the city, you go along Skeppsbron. You know, on the other side of Old Town? That’s great.

>>>Oh, that side, right? That’s really beautiful.

Yeah. And the moon is shining out. Yeah, all the lights and the water.

Do you want to get married? Do you want children? What do you want to do with them? How do you want them to grow up?

A9
Yeah. Well…. (thinking)… I think I hope we can… (thinking)… how shall I put it? I hope they grow up and feel that we love them and that we care for them. And that they become caring and thinking and… (thinking) I hope I can help them cope with reality and the facts of reality.

You know, they could understand each other.

Q11
What was the happiest moment in your life? What was the most terrifying moment in your life?

A11
When my kids were born. That’s a standard? When you know they’re okay and their mothers as well. It’s a great feeling. I think that’s my best experience, actually. And my worst? (Thinking)… Well. That’s very private. Or personal, maybe I should say? …I guess one very dramatic moment was when one of the kids swallowed a piece of plastic when he was a toddler and almost suffocated. And we had to call an ambulance but he was okay. You know, when some things really happen, you have this intense focus. It’s like your senses are really sharpened and also you have this different perception of time. Goes really slow. And well, this is quite dramatic and at the moment, when it happened, it’s—you don’t think. You just react. But the fear, and all the feelings, they come afterward. I guess that’s my worst moment.

>>>Interesting.

But… (Thinking)… I also think of the moment when I understood that my father was dead. That my father had died.

>>>What do you mean by understood?

Yeah, because he died in an accident and the police called me, and told me they had found a body. That was also when I realized that it had been my father. So that was another really bad moment.

>>>That is terrifying. Was it a car accident?

No. No, it was a house that burnt down. It was fire.

>>>…I think we should move on to the next one.

Yeah.


Q12
What was your childhood dream? What would you say to the childhood you, now? And what is your dream now?

A12
(Thinking) Well, I don’t know if I have a goal like that. If I can keep on working as an artist, I’ll be fine. That’s great. And what would I say to myself?

>>>So your childhood dream was to become an artist?

No, I don’t think I had one. (Thinking)… No, I think I wanted to be a rock star, actually. (Both laugh) Yeah, but I’m too shy.

>>>Oh. There’s probably a few shy rock stars out there?

Yeah, I know but you have to have some kind of drive too. I played in some bands but it wasn’t really my thing. I’m kind of… I like to sit in my studio and just be all alone. (laughs)

>>>Not in front of 20,000 people. (laughs)

No, no. And, you know, a friend of mine said “Art is exhibitionism for the shy”. And I think that’s true. You know, you present an object instead of yourself. And what was the last question?

>>>So what would you tell yourself?

I would tell myself you’ll be okay. You won’t become a rock star but it’ll be okay. (Both laugh)
