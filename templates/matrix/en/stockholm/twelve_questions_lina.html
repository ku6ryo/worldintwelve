Lina Karlsson

Profile and Leo's Take
Age：28　Years in Stockholm: 9

Lina was introduced to me through Asuka. I met Lina a couple of times, once when she invited me to a midsummer party hosted by a digital marketing company, and the second time when we had lunch at a park near her work (and did the questions). Lina is my defining image of a cool Swedish young person living in Stockholm; she’s smart, has educated opinions, is cultured, and is super friendly. Yeah, its hard to beat! Talking with her on both occasions was great (I still remember our conversation on relationships lol), but the way she answered the questions were really revealing too. Having that unbiased, objective sense of Sweden and its position in the world, it’s just something you don’t see in many other people. So yeah, I highly recommend reading all her answers, but if I were to choose, it would be the images (Q.5), the future (Q.6) which is fascinating, marriage/children (Q.9), and the moments (Q.11). So Lina, thanks for all your hospitality, and I will definitely see you again! Come to Tokyo, or maybe in New York…??

Q6
How do you feel about the future? Both yourself and mankind.

A6
Just regular thoughts? Most of all, I'm feeling worried. Hmm…not just talking about… not just Sweden. Everywhere. You’d think in 2015 we'd be somewhere else with regards to sexism, racism, social... the class differences. Everything that's bad about society is just getting worse, I think. …In some ways, yeah, people are getting more and more educated, but people are also getting more and more stupid. So, yeah, that worries me a lot, actually. Where are we going? How can people understand simple things like how to just take care of each other? In Sweden, we have this party called the Swedish Democrats. They say they're not racist, they’re not this and that, but they basically don't want any more people to be let in Sweden. And it's so fucked up. To me, it's so fucked up. How can you think that when people are running from war and stuff? They have nowhere to go. How can you be so selfish? We're sitting here. We have one of the world's best lifestyles. The gap between rich and poor here is so tiny. The standard for us is so good, and we don't want to share any of that. It's so…I mean, beyond egoistic. You can't put yourself in anyone else's shoes just because you're born in a country -- it doesn't make sense.

And talking about racism in America, for example, how Black people are still getting treated...or, not just Black people, but everybody not perceived as white or whatever. That's so weird that we're not... We don't have more knowledge about this yet. That concerns me a lot. To the future, yeah. That concerns me.

>>> Do you think this has to do with... well being here for a while, I feel like everything in Sweden, from the law to customs, are based heavily around the concept of equality.

Equality, but, still, people don't know that we're racist in this country, and that's how racist we are.

>>>Very very interesting.

There's still a big difference between being...well, speaking Swedish fluently and if you don't, or you're a woman or you're a man or you're disabled. All of these things. Homosexual or heterosexual.
