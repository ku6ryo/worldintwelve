Mathias Eriksson

Profile and Leo's Take
Age：26　Years in Stockholm: 24

Mathias I met at his (now previous) company’s summer party, that Lina invited me to. (The company was a digital design agency, and Mathias himself is a designer). So I actually met Mathias twice during my stay in Stockhom, and the second time I actually had him come along for one of my chores (and a drink) after his work. Talking with Mathias was interesting because it really gave me a glimpse of the thoughts and ideas held by the young crowd in Stockholm, and a bigger picture, Sweden. All the marvels of Swedish social security and governmental structure are really not geared towards the mid-twenty single population, and I was witnessing this as an outsider. Talking with Msathias about these subjects, and just general things regarding the future and goals, all led to an enlightening experience. For his answers, I recommend the future (Q.6), the thing (Q.7), children (Q.9), and the moments (Q.11). So Mathias, we’ll talk again (I MUST get this book contract going! haha), and if you’re in the mood, head over to Tokyo! Or maybe we can meet in LA…that would be interesting :)

Check out Mathias' work at www.mathiaseriksson.com. Some pretty big name clients...

Q6
How do you feel about the future? Both yourself and mankind.

A6
[For myself] I guess “forever undecided” is something that comes up, and something I strive for personally is to not have a kind of set out path or set out goal. I guess in general, mankind seems to be always be in that changing mode as well. You never really know what's around the corner, so maybe that's what makes it interesting, I don't know. 

 >>>Well is it positive or negative or is it truly undecided? 

 Yeah, probably just undecided. You don't really know. Yeah, maybe I'm cynical like that.  We'll see. What happens happens. 

 I don't worry too much about it because it's going to here soon enough and all you can do is your best to influence it, but it's going to get here.  But it's like mankind, it's a big thing but there's something interesting just happening with connectivity and global matters and it seems from what one point of view it's interesting how the world maybe speeds up a little bit more for each generation. Like if you think about communication, how slow that was fifty years ago, a hundred years ago, a hundred fifty years ago for trends, opinions, and in general, people’s awareness.  It depends on how you look on it.  Sometimes you can be amazed at how people are aware all over the globe, at the same time, about one thing. However, something shitty can happen, done by somebody or a group of people, and you just realize that there are many people who aren't on that same level of awareness. So I think it's always about a different pace.  I think that it's going to be a struggle for the ones that are in the “fast lane” to understand the people who aren't, if that makes any sense at all, I don't know.  It makes sense in here [Points at head]. 

 >>>I think we, and people reading sort of get what you're saying though.  It's true and the whole aspect of speed of information, speed of connectivity is a big deal, so... basically you're saying where the hell is all this technology gonna take us next. 

 Yeah.  (Laughs)  Exactly.

 >>>That's very unique.  That's very true too.

 >>>Very true.

It was like well, whatever.”  It's like nobody asks you “how's it been, it's been ok?”  We never really get into any details like that.  But it’s cool that you could have those kind of parallel lives, so it makes it more accessible to go away.
