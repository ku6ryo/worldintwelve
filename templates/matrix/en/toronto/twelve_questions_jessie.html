Jessie Sheng

Profile and Leo's Take
Age：24　Years in Toronto： 6

Jessie was introduced to me through Desmond. Jessie is one of those girls with one of those charming personalities and wonderful smiles. She was also super kind enough to meet up with me a couple of times, helping out on various projects, and showing me around OCAD (Ontario College of Art and Design). OCAD itself has to be one of the weirder looking/architecturally bizarre buildings in not just Toronto but the whole world, which is kind of ironic because Jessie turned from a life of working after her architectural studies at U of T to creating animation at OCAD (which frankly, to me, seems like an awesome decision :) ) Jessie gave great answers throughout, and if I were to choose a number, they would be the flag (Q.4), the future (Q.6), her partner (Q.8), and her moments (Q.11). Jessie, see you around, and do come to Tokyo again when I’m there!!

Q6
How do you feel about the future? Both yourself and mankind.

A6
Um… I feel excited for the future. I think overall we're heading to good places and… it could be really interesting. I'm really interested in seeing what kind of technology emerges in the future and how it changes our lives. The computer only happened what a couple of decades ago? And look how much it changed everything and how we do things and little cultural things, and also how are people going to dress in the future? Will people look back today and be "Oh yeah 2000s, that was such a weird period. Let's have year 2000s party where we all dress up like we're from the 2000s!" (Laughs) Also I'm really interested in how the present looks to the future. Because you don't understand things until you've gotten past them. And… and yeah I think——well at least I'm hoping, that through social activism and whatnot, things will change for the better. I think I see that now, so I guess I'm excited to see those things play out into… better producing good results. A better future.



Q7
What's the most important "thing" for you?

A7
That’s a tricky question. Hmm… I think as a person, your outlook on life is very important. Like, being able to see the bigger picture and where you fit into all of it, as well as appreciating the richness in mundane experiences. I used to keep a blog where I would write about the beauty in random stuff like broken plates, dry spaghetti, and even shower water. It was a lot of fun, but basically, it was about discovering happiness in simple pleasures. The world is shaped by how we see it, so I think it’s really valuable to see the good where there is good as well as ways in which things could always be better.
