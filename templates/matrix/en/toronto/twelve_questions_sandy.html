Profile and Leo's Take
Age：37　Years in Toronto： 20

Sandy I met at Han and Bernard’s party. My first impression of Sandy was “Holy…this man is BUILT!!!” It turned out Sandy was actually on Canada’s national dragon boat team, and he had just come to the party after practice. It explains why he was equal in presence to the Greek God he is posing next to in his banner pic haha. I even included a picture I took with Sandy to prove this!! You can check out a lot of this in his questions, and if I were to choose a few, they would be the closeness (Q.2), the future (Q.6), the moments (Q.11), and the dreams (Q.12). Q1
If you were handed US$2500 and received a one month vacation where you could live in one city, anywhere in the world, where would you choose? What would you do?

A1
At this moment in time, I would say I would like to visit Dubai. I grew up there and things have changed since I came to Canada. It’s not the Dubai that I know it to be. So probably revisiting my childhood place where I grew up and exploring the area. I’m not too sure if $2,500 will cut it for a month in that city but I’d probably try to fund it a little bit myself as well.

>>>How long were you in Dubai?

I was there for about seven years. Yes. It was pretty much the “wonder years.” When I was growing up, since I was about five. So those experiences have lasted with me this long, and I never had an opportunity to go back and take a look at the city.


Q2
What country do you feel “close” to, other than your own?

A2
(Thinking)…I guess I almost answered that with my first question. I would say I was very closely associated with Dubai. More so than Sri Lanka as well. I am Sri Lankan but I think most of my childhood memories and what I remember and what I cherish more are my experiences in Dubai and that’s why I associate myself with Dubai. The United Arab Emirates.

>>>Did you have a specific experience in Dubai that you cherish?

I think the schools, which they have this really good system that I went to. It was just free. It’s a city that you don’t have much to worry about as a kid. As an adult, I think there are other issues but as a kid, it felt safe. Innocent environment when I was there.

>>>Yeah because I think most people probably reading this or watching this have no notion of what Dubai in the 80’s was like. (laughs) And so that’s why I asked that.

Right. And it was a very innocent city and innocent time for a lot of kids growing up. I can’t say much about the adults. I was too young to comprehend the situation.

Q6
How do you feel about the future? Both yourself and mankind.

A6
Okay. I find that if I were to assess future of the average Canadian, I feel like they’re going to be poorer. I feel that they’re not that... In 20 years, the people who are 37 years old would likely be able to afford less. And that has to do with the cost of living rising and a whole bunch of other issues but it’s just that we’ve taken for granted the…. (thinking) How do I put this? We take it for granted how far we’ve progressed compared to third-world nations, and the reason why we are able to afford certain things is because there are third-world nations. That third-world nations are willing to be paid less to produce stuff that we consume. As soon as these third-world countries start coming out of that situation, where their cost of living rises, their lifestyle improves, then we’re not going to have the opportunity to get things from those countries for the price that we were paying. It’s gonna come to a point where I find Canadians, North Americans, are going to find life not as comfortable. That’s just because of economics itself.

Then you talk about the environment. I see that there’s a push to get the environment on the “good side.” Or to get on the environment’s “good side.” But I think that… (thinking)… I think the salvation is more like some sort of innovation versus what we seem to be attempting now, which is conservation. Conservation doesn’t seem to be working as fast as we need it to. So the only way the environment can get better, I find, is if somebody comes up with some amazing idea that would sort of fix it.

Having said that, I like to have an optimistic view of what the future holds. So I was just referring to the next 50 years but I’m always a person that’s always been into exploring the stars. Love to look. I love to see the Sci-Fi aspect of it. And that’s something I’d love humanity to get towards. But the next 50 years are gonna be quite trying, I think.

Q10
What would be the ideal thing to see the first moment you wake up? What would be the ideal thing to see the moment before you fall asleep?

A10
So when I wake up, the one thing that I want to be is relaxed. That has a deeper impact than just a word. To be relaxed, it just simply means that you have no worries. You’ve dealt with all your issues the previous night. You have no concerns ahead of you other than your day to day ritual but there’s nothing that’s in the back of your mind that’s gonna be building up into anxiety. And similarly, that’s the thing that’ll help me sleep. So going to bed, what I want, is that clarity that I’ve dealt with all the issues that are gonna pop up and there’s not gonna be a bad surprise in the future. So I think going to bed relaxed, waking up relaxed, is probably the key thing for me.

>>>There’s no really one thing?

There’s isn’t. For me, it’s that feeling. The feeling of being relaxed. The state of being relaxed and that’s connected to quite a lot of things. It’s hard to achieve. There’s always gonna be something. Maybe work, people you’re around. Just the weather. It’s tricky.



Q11
What was the happiest moment in your life? What was the most terrifying moment in your life?

A11
Hmm… I’d say one of the most happiest moments probably go back to a Christmas that I had and this is me being a kid. That it really is the happiness in getting stuff and getting toys. So it’s a very superficial sense but it still rings strong in my mind. There was a Christmas and I think it was 1986 perhaps where my parents basically wanted to give us the Christmas of our life. And they spent as much as they could. We were very poor but they tried to provide us with this experience that a kid should experience once in their life. One Christmas where “oh my goodness, there’s chocolate after chocolate. There’s toys after toys.” And they’re small toys but as a kid, you don’t understand value. You don’t understand quality. You understand quantity more than anything else. And me and my brother, we had a great time that year.

But I think it’s also if you look back at reflection, it was one of the few times that our family was together as well, at that time. So the reason I’m bringing that up is because in hindsight, I recognize it as a closer family that I had. So I hope that explains a little bit of that question.

The second half was the terrifying side. That is tricky. I don’t… it’s interesting because it’s hard for me to recollect the most the terrifying thing. I can have a terrifying experience just driving and getting stopped by a cop because I was speeding. That itself just gets my heart pounding. But it’s not a worthy experience, I’d say. It does get me terrified but I won’t give it the time of day. So I would say…(thinking) There was such an anxiety when I came to this country. We took refuge in Canada and there’s a time when we didn’t know if our application would be accepted or not. Canada was gonna be the place where we made our home as a family. So…terrifying, anxious, yes. That was the “make or break” decision of where my life was gonna end up. And they said yes. Canada said yes and I made a great life out of it. But if they had said no, my life could have been somewhere else altogether.

>>>Wow. That is a big moment of your life.

That is the making or breaking of my life. Yep.

And it’s about really enjoying a little bit of what we have here.
