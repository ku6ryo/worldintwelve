12 Stops × 12 Animals

Overview



This is a sub-project where one quantity of 12 is crossed with another quantity of 12 to create representation of that city. In this one, I choose a park I want to visit, get on a train or a bus, go 12 stops, get off, and walk to the rest of the way. While walking, I take photographs of the first 12 animals I see. Hence: "12 Stops × 12 Animals"



Route



So for Toronto, I started at St. Clair Station which was the closes stop to where I was living, and went 12 stops from there to St. Patrick Station.



My final destination was Queen's Park in University of Toronto, but fearin there wouldn't be too many animals on a weekday in February, I decided to go to Grange Park too.




After getting out of the station, I went past OCAD and to the park.

Afterthoughts


So I couldn't find 12 animals in my initial journey, but I believe things ended up pretty good. One intereseting thing I found were dog parks. You see them in New York too, a little, but its basically proof that there is limited space for dogs to run around in major metropoli.





Last Words


The biggest "What the...??" moments in Toronto regarding animals were all the dogs and their socks/shoes. It makes sense, since they are walking on snow all day and their feet do get pretty dirty. At least that's what I think they are for. Also I just thought there wer eprobably less animals to see in general because of the season I went, and that the summer would be quite different.
